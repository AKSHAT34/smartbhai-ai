Key Technical Decisions

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): System prompt injects actual portfolio holdings, sector allocation, options chain data, stock-specific options, and all 9 market indicators. Prevents hallucination — AI can't fabricate numbers because real data is in the prompt.

Options Chain Simplification — The "4 Key Levels"

A raw options chain has 15+ columns × 30+ strikes — completely overwhelming for Tier-2/3 investors. Our AI distills it into 4 actionable numbers:

#LevelWhat It IsPrototype ValuePlain-English Meaning
1🧲 Max PainPrice where most option buyers lose money. Market gravitates here near expiry.22,450"NIFTY ka gravity point — expiry pe yahan aane ki koshish karta hai"
2🟢 Support (Highest Put OI)Strike with most put open interest. Big money sold puts here = confident market won't fall below.22,600"Floor — bade players ne yahan bet lagaya hai ki market neeche nahi jayega"
3🔴 Resistance (Highest Call OI)Strike with most call open interest. Big money sold calls here = don't expect market above this.22,500"Ceiling — bade players ne yahan bet lagaya hai ki market upar nahi jayega"
4💎 IV PercentileAre options cheap or expensive vs history? <25% = cheap (buy). >75% = expensive (sell).62% (moderate)"Sale season indicator — low IV = options on sale, buy protection"

AI output from these 4 levels: "Market expects NIFTY to stay between 22,400-22,600. PCR at 0.93 is neutral. Options moderately priced. If bullish → Bull Call Spread 22,400-22,500. If want protection → buy 22,200 PE at ₹62." — Entire options chain in one paragraph.

Adaptive Profiling without ML training: Weighted signal formula: (risky×3 + moderate×1.5) / (safe×2 + moderate×1.5 + risky×3 + 1) × 100. Score smoothed with 30% previous + 70% new. Classifies: Safe (0-29), Moderate (30-64), Risky (65-100).

6-Model Fallback Chain: Llama 3.3 70B → Gemma 27B → Gemma 12B → Mistral Small 24B → Gemma 4B → Llama 3.2 3B. Rotates starting index on success. Zero cost, zero downtime.

Risk Gate System: 6 regex patterns detect dangerous intent (naked selling, all-in, short straddle) in real-time as user types. Color-coded warning banner appears before sending. System prompt forces AI to lead with warnings.

SEBI Compliance: 7 hard rules in system prompt — no direct buy/sell recommendations, no guaranteed returns, mandatory disclaimers, max-loss-first framing, naked selling protocol.

Options Strategies Implemented

StrategyTypeDefinitionWhen to UseRisk
Bull Call SpreadBuyBuy a lower-strike call + sell a higher-strike call at the same expiry. Caps both profit and loss.Mildly bullish — expect moderate upsideLimited: net premium paid
Bear Put SpreadBuyBuy a higher-strike put + sell a lower-strike put. Profits when market falls.Mildly bearish — expect moderate downsideLimited: net premium paid
Long Call / Long PutBuyBuy a call (bullish) or put (bearish) outright. Simplest options trade.Strong directional viewLimited: full premium paid (but 100% of it)
Iron CondorBuy+SellSell a call spread + sell a put spread simultaneously. 4 legs. Earn premium if market stays in a range.Neutral — expect low volatility, range-bound marketLimited: difference between strikes minus premium
Butterfly SpreadBuy+SellBuy 1 lower call + sell 2 middle calls + buy 1 higher call. Max profit if market pins at middle strike.Pinpoint view — expect market to settle at a specific priceLimited: very low net premium
Protective PutBuyBuy a put on stocks you already own. Acts as insurance against a fall.Hold stocks but fear a crashLimited: premium paid (insurance cost)
Covered CallSellSell a call on stocks you already own. Earn premium income. If stock rises above strike, shares get called away.Hold stocks, want income, mildly bullish/neutralOpportunity cost: miss upside above strike. No cash loss.
Credit SpreadSellSell a higher-premium option + buy a lower-premium option at a further strike. Collect net credit upfront.Directional view with defined riskLimited: difference between strikes minus credit
Cash-Secured PutSellSell a put while keeping enough cash to buy the stock if assigned. Get paid to wait for a lower entry price.Want to buy a stock cheaper + earn income while waitingMust buy stock at strike if it falls — but you wanted to anyway
CollarSell+BuyOwn stock + buy a protective put + sell a covered call. The call premium offsets the put cost.Protect gains on a winning stock at near-zero costLimited upside (capped at call strike), limited downside (put protects)
